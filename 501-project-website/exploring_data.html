Where rumors spreads?

By visulizing the distribution of 11179 rumors, we can find that rumors exist everywhere. No matter how popular the domain is, there are rumors on it. Additionally, rumors are more likely to appear on relatively popular domains, which is a bit counterintuitive.

Data set used: labeled_rumor_python.csv

What about the author of rumors?

Signed rumors are much more than anonymous rumors. Among 11179 rumors, more than 9000 rumors can be traced. We are able to find who created these texts rapidly and easily. However, there are still more than 2000 rumors are anonymous.

Data set used: labeled_rumor_python.csv

What's the content of rumors?

Political topics are the most common topic of rumors. Celebrities and countries are often used when rumormongers are writing rumors.

Data set used: labeled_rumor_python.csv

What's people's reaction to rumors?

The visualization of the numbers of likes and shares of rumors are rather wierd, but it is what happened in the real world. Although there are so many rumors, all of these rumors are neither widely shared or liked. The maxiumns of both metrics are only 2.

Data set used: labeled_rumor_python.csv

As a contrast, what's people's reaction to real events?

For real events, the distribution of numbers of likes and shares are much more than the ones of rumors. They are also more various. Besides, the numbers of likes and the numbers of shares have a positive relationship in general.

Data set used: labeled_truth_R.csv

Method Used:

The visualization is created by using python packages "Seaborn" and "Wordcloud".

Process:

The EDA was conducted after the raw data sets have been cleaned and the feature engineering has been finished.
