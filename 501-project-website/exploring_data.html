Analysis on Accounts Sending Rumors

How much impact they have?

According to my data, the accounts with relatively low number of followers are the most. 60 out of 100 accounts was followed by less than 40,000 people. However, there still exsit accounts with huge influnce, which means account with high number of followers are still possibly sending rumors.

Data set used: cleaned_rumor_manually.csv

Are they verified?

Among these acounts, 25% accounts are verified. Considering the limitation of being verfied on Twitter, the rate is relatively high. This means, even an account seems to be official and authoritative, it is also possible for it to publish rumors.

Data set used: cleaned_rumor_manually.csv

How their descriptions are written?

Many account ask for freedom in their descriptions. It is quite often for such accounts to ask people follow them. Hoster of specific insitution is quite normal in this group, which is the reason why there are so many verified accounts. In addtion, politics is the topic mentioned the most in their descriptions.

Data set used: cleaned_rumor_manually.csv


Analysis on Rumors

What's the content of rumors?

Russo-Ukrainian War, Covid and election is the main topic of rumors. Since all the rumors collected appeared in 2022, it is obvious that the content of rumors tend to be the recent hotspots. Besides, celebrities, like Bill Gates, are also a popular part of rumors. "Breaking" is frequently used in writing rumors.

Data set used: cleaned_rumor_manually.csv

What's people's reaction to rumors?

Most rumors were shared with less than 500000 times and were liked with less than 20000 times. Some rumors are widely spread.

Data set used: cleaned_rumor_manually.csv

As a contrast, what's people's reaction to real events?

For real events, the stats are low. This makes sense since rumors are always related to important events and are full of emotion, while real events are always boring.

Data set used: labeled_truth_R.csv
